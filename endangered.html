The various animals

In 2019, the Trump administration announced several rollbacks to the Endangered Species Act. The changes made it easier to remove animals from legally protected status and allowed commercial projects to take place on land previously deemed critical habitat. This policy only impacts conservation efforts in the United States. But around the world, ecosystems and wildlife face growing threats from human activities, habitat loss and climate change. If one species dies off, its predators don't have as much to eat — and the cycle repeats. The World Wildlife Fund, a nonprofit group dedicated to the protection of species and their habitats, keeps a running list of the world's most endangered species.



South Hopper Penguin

Conservation status: Vulnerable

In the wild, you'll find these small penguins on the coasts of Argentina, Australia, Chile and the Falkland Islands. But in the past three decades, their population has dropped 25%, as climate change threatens their habitats. Here, rockhopper penguins waddle — and, yes, hop — around a zoo in Munich, Germany



Snow Leopard

Conservation status: Vulnerable

There are an estimated 4,080 to 6,590 snow leopards left in the wild. Those few thousand cats are spread across the high mountains of 12 countries including China, Nepal, Afghanistan, Russia and Mongolia.
